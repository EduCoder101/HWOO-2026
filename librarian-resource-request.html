Library Resource Request

Year 5 Geography: Place & Space – World Heritage Investigation (Lessons 16–20)

Our Year 5 class is studying 10 World Heritage Sites as part of our geography unit on Place & Space. Students research the significance, management, and threats facing each site, then write a comparative report on two sites.

We would love to borrow any books, atlases, or reference materials you can find on the sites listed below. Books aimed at upper primary or lower secondary reading levels are ideal. We need these resources for approximately 3–4 weeks.

Even one or two books per site would be a huge help. If a site is too obscure for our collection, please skip it and we will supplement with online sources.

World Heritage Site Location What students need to find out
Great Barrier Reef
Oceania (Australia)		 Queensland, Australia Why it was listed, coral bleaching and climate threats, marine management strategies, Indigenous connections to the reef
Uluru-Kata Tjuta
Oceania (Australia)		 Northern Territory, Australia Cultural significance to Anangu people, joint management with traditional owners, tourism management, geological formation
Wet Tropics of Queensland
Oceania (Australia)		 Queensland, Australia Rainforest biodiversity, Indigenous land management, threats from development and climate change, conservation strategies
Machu Picchu
South America		 Peru Inca civilisation and purpose of the site, discovery and preservation, tourism pressure, environmental threats (landslides, erosion)
Great Wall of China
Asia		 China Historical purpose and construction, different sections and their condition, preservation challenges, tourism and development pressure
Serengeti National Park
Africa		 Tanzania Wildlife migration patterns, ecosystem significance, poaching and habitat threats, conservation and community involvement
Galápagos Islands
South America		 Ecuador Unique species and Darwin’s research, marine and land ecosystems, invasive species threats, visitor management strategies
Angkor Wat
Asia		 Cambodia Khmer civilisation and temple complex, religious and cultural significance, restoration work, tourism and water table threats
Venice and its Lagoon
Europe		 Italy Historical importance as a maritime city, flooding and sea-level rise, MOSE barrier project, mass tourism challenges
Rapa Nui (Easter Island)
Oceania (South Pacific)		 Chile Moai statues and Polynesian culture, environmental history and deforestation, isolation and preservation challenges, Indigenous land rights

Any general books on World Heritage Sites, UNESCO, or conservation would also be useful as shared class references.
